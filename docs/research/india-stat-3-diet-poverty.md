# India: Child Diet Poverty & Malnutrition Statistics

**Research date:** June 2026  
**Focus:** Current, credible statistics on child hunger, malnutrition, and inadequate dietary access in India from authoritative sources.

---

## Sourced Statistics

### 1. Child Stunting
**Statistic:** 35.5% of children under 5 years are stunted  
**Source:** National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Decline from 38.4% in NFHS-4 (2015–16), but remains critically high. India has the largest number of stunted children globally at 46.6 million.

### 2. Child Wasting
**Statistic:** 19.3% of children under 5 years are wasted  
**Source:** National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Slight improvement from earlier surveys. India has the largest number of wasted children globally at 25.5 million. Global average wasting is 6.6%.

### 3. Child Underweight
**Statistic:** 32.1% of children under 5 years are underweight  
**Source:** National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Decline from 35.8% in NFHS-4 (2015–16). Indicates chronic inadequate nutrition intake.

### 4. Anaemia in Children
**Statistic:** 64% of children aged 6 months and above are anaemic  
**Source:** National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Increase from 55% in NFHS-4 (2015–16). State variation: Gujarat 80%, Maharashtra 69%, Assam 68%, Jharkhand 67%.

### 5. Minimum Acceptable Diet (Children 6–23 Months)
**Statistic:** Only 17.8% of children aged 6–23 months receive an adequate diet (minimum acceptable diet)  
**Source:** National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Significant regional variation. National average masks severe disparities: Odisha 40%, Assam 24%, Mizoram 34%, Gujarat 16%, Jharkhand 22%.

### 6. Multidimensional Child Poverty
**Statistic:** 27% of children were multidimensionally poor  
**Source:** National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5)  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Decline from 38% in NFHS-4 (2015–16), but 27% still represents ~90 million children in poverty, many unable to afford or access healthy diets.

### 7. Malnutrition as Cause of Under-5 Deaths
**Statistic:** Malnutrition accounts for 68.2% of all under-5 deaths in India  
**Source:** Global Burden of Disease Study (Indian state analysis)  
**Year:** 2017 (most recent comprehensive analysis)  
**Context:** Malnutrition was the predominant risk factor for death in children under 5 across every Indian state.

### 8. Severe Wasting in Global Context
**Statistic:** India has 20 million severely wasted children—50% of the world's severely wasted children  
**Source:** UNICEF/Global Nutrition Report data synthesized via NFHS-5  
**Year:** 2019–2021  
**Context:** Demonstrates the scale and severity of India's child nutrition crisis despite economic growth.

### 9. Global Hunger Index Ranking
**Statistic:** India ranks 102nd out of 123 countries (GHI score: 25.8 = "serious" hunger level)  
**Source:** Global Hunger Index  
**Year:** 2025  
**Context:** Child stunting 32.9%, child wasting 18.7%, child undernutrition prevalence 12.0%, child mortality 2.8%.

---

## Key Takeaways for Fundraising Narrative

1. **Scale:** ~350 million children under 5 in India; 35.5% stunted, 19.3% wasted, 32.1% underweight. Only 17.8% eating adequately.
2. **Anaemia crisis:** 64% of children 6+ months anaemic (iron, B12, folate deficiency).
3. **Death rate:** Malnutrition is the leading cause of under-5 mortality (68.2% of deaths).
4. **Poverty-diet link:** 27% of children multidimensionally poor; unable to access diverse, nutritious foods.
5. **Global burden:** 50% of world's severely wasted children live in India.
