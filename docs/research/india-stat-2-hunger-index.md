## Summary: India's Hunger & Malnutrition Crisis (2024–2025)

**The Challenge:**
- **102nd globally** on GHI (2025), "serious" hunger severity category
- **40.6% of population** (456 million) live in moderate or severe food insecurity
- **18.7% of under-5 children** experience acute wasting — highest rate in the world
- **32.9–35.5% of under-5 children** are stunted (chronic undernutrition)
- **172 million undernourished people** despite steady decline from 243 million in 2006
- **74% unable to afford** a healthy diet

**Positive Trends:**
- Undernourishment prevalence declined from 16.6% (2020–22) to 12.0% (2021–23)
- Under-5 mortality halved from 1990 baseline; SDG target on track
- India achieves 42% reduction in under-5 mortality since SDGs launched (2015)

**The Core Problem:**
Not food shortage, but dysfunctional distribution and post-harvest infrastructure—a perfect opportunity for a local, direct-to-kitchen meal-and-photo donation model like FeedSomeone.
