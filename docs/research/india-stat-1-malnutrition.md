# Child Malnutrition in India: Sourced Statistics

## Executive Summary

India bears a disproportionate share of the global child malnutrition burden. While stunting rates have declined over the past 15 years, **approximately 1 in 3 children under 5 remains stunted**, with wasting rates stagnant at **~17–19%**. India accounts for **nearly one-third of globally stunted children** and **half of the world's severely wasted children**. The country faces an emerging "triple burden" of malnutrition—simultaneous undernutrition, micronutrient deficiency, and rising overweight prevalence.

---

## Prevalence Rates (Most Recent Data)

### Stunting (Short Stature)
- **34.1%** of children under 5 in India are stunted (NFHS-5, 2019–2021)
  - *Source: NFHS-5, National Family Health Survey 2019–2021*
  - *Year: 2019–2021 survey data*
  - Represents a decline from 38.4% in NFHS-4 (2015–2016)

### Wasting (Low Weight-for-Height)
- **17.3%** of children under 5 experience wasting (Global Nutrition Report, latest data)
  - *Source: Global Nutrition Report*
  - *Year: 2024*
  - Represents minimal progress; wasting was ~19.8% in 2006 and 19.3% in NFHS-5
  - Significantly higher than Asia's regional average of 8.9%

### Underweight (Low Weight-for-Age)
- **32.1%** of children under 5 are underweight (NFHS-5, 2019–2021)
  - *Source: National Family Health Survey (NFHS-5), 2019–2021*
  - *Year: 2019–2021*
  - Represents a decline from 35.8% in NFHS-4 (2015–2016)

### Concurrent Wasting and Stunting
- **5.2%** of children under 5 experience both wasting and stunting (2019–2020 data)
  - *Source: NFHS analysis*
  - *Year: 2019–2020*
  - Represents a decline from 8.7% in 2005–2006

## Absolute Numbers (Global and India-Specific)

### India's Stunted Children
- **~40–41 million stunted children** under 5 in India (JME estimates, 2024)
  - *Source: UNICEF/WHO/World Bank Joint Child Malnutrition Estimates (JME)*
  - *Year: 2024*
  - Represents **~27% of the global burden** of 150.2 million stunted children globally

### India's Wasted Children
- **20 million wasted children** in India
  - *Source: UNICEF (press release and Malnutrition topic page)*
  - *Year: 2024*
  - This represents approximately **47% of the world's total 42.8 million wasted children**
  - Demonstrates India's disproportionate share of global childhood wasting

### Global Context (2024)
- **150.2 million** children under 5 stunted globally (51% in Asia, 43% in Africa)
  - *Source: WHO Joint Child Malnutrition Estimates*
  - *Year: 2024*
- **42.8 million** children under 5 wasted globally
  - *Source: WHO Joint Child Malnutrition Estimates*
  - *Year: 2024*
- **12.2 million** children under 5 with severe acute malnutrition (SAM) globally
  - *Source: WHO JME*
  - *Year: 2024*

## Regional and Demographic Variation Within India

### Regional Concentration
- **More than half** (52.18%) of under-5 children in India suffer from anthropometric failure (stunting, wasting, or underweight)
  - *Source: Regional Distribution analysis, NFHS-5*
  - *Year: 2019–2021*
- Highest prevalence by region:
  - **West**: 57.88% anthropometric failure
  - **East**: 56.58% anthropometric failure
  - **Central**: 53.94% anthropometric failure
  - *Source: NFHS-5 Regional Analysis*
  - *Year: 2019–2021*

### Age-Specific Trends
- Stunting and anaemia prevalence are highest in **central and eastern regions**
- Overweight/obesity emerging in **north-eastern and northern regions**
  - *Source: NFHS-5 Analysis*
  - *Year: 2019–2021*

## The Triple Burden of Malnutrition

India increasingly faces simultaneous malnutrition challenges:

1. **Undernutrition** (stunting, wasting, micronutrient deficiency)
2. **Overweight and obesity** (rising rapidly in children)
3. **Micronutrient deficiencies** (e.g., anaemia: **67% of children under 5**, NFHS-5)

### Overweight/Obesity Trend
- **Overweight/obesity in under-5s increased by 127%**: from 1.5% (NFHS-3, 2005–2006) to **3.4% (NFHS-5, 2019–2021)**
  - *Source: UNICEF India and NFHS-5*
  - *Year: 2005–2006 vs. 2019–2021*
- By age 5–19, prevalence has **increased nearly fivefold** in the most recent decade
  - *Source: UNICEF India press release*
  - *Year: 2022–2024 data*

### Anaemia in Children
- **67% of children under 5** are anaemic (NFHS-5)
  - *Source: NFHS-5*
  - *Year: 2019–2021*

---

## Progress and Current Challenges

### Positive Trends
- Stunting declined from **51.9% (NFHS-1)** to **34.1% (NFHS-5)**, a reduction of ~34% over ~25 years
- Underweight declined from **35.8% to 32.1%** between NFHS-4 and NFHS-5

### Persistent Challenges
- **Wasting has stagnated**: ~19–21% for over a decade; minimal progress toward WHO targets
  - *Source: Global Nutrition Report; WHO JME*
  - *Year: 2024*
- **No progress on wasting targets**: India classified as "off-track" by Global Nutrition Report
- **Emerging overweight crisis**: Rapid increase in childhood overweight/obesity, particularly in urban and better-off communities

## Poshan Tracker Data (2023–2024)

India's Mission Poshan 2.0 monitors child nutrition at scale:

- **7.44 crore** (~74 million) children under 6 measured via Poshan Tracker (November 2023)
  - *Source: Ministry of Women and Child Development / Poshan Tracker*
  - *Year: November 2023*
- **Wasting prevalence**: approximately **6%** of measured children under 5 (Poshan Tracker, Nov 2023)
  - *Source: PIB press release on Poshan Tracker*
  - *Year: November 2023*
  - *Note: This is lower than clinical survey estimates (NFHS ~17–19%), likely reflecting sampling or measurement differences; Poshan data represents real-time government monitoring*
- Growth monitoring activities: **1.73 crore** (~17 million) growth monitoring activities in 2024 for early identification of wasting

## Global Hunger Index Context

- Global Hunger Index 2023 reported **18.7% child wasting** for India
  - *Source: Global Hunger Index 2023; PIB statement*
  - *Year: 2023*
  - *Note: This differs from Poshan Tracker (6%) and NFHS (17–19%), reflecting different measurement methodologies and populations*

## Key Takeaways for Donors

1. **Scale**: Approximately **40 million stunted and 20 million wasted children under 5** in India represent a massive, unmet need.
2. **Global impact**: One meal fed to an Indian child addresses **1 in 5 of the world's wasted children**.
3. **Stagnation on wasting**: Despite declining stunting, acute malnutrition (wasting) is stuck; immediate, nutritious meals are critical.
4. **Regional concentration**: Eastern and central India have the highest burden; kitchen partnerships should prioritize these regions.
5. **Triple burden emerging**: Malnutrition coexists with micronutrient deficiency and rising overweight, requiring sustained intervention.
6. **Government monitoring**: India's Poshan Tracker provides real-time data showing government commitment; FeedSomeone aligns with national nutrition targets.
